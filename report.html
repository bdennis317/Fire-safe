Local Regulations & Compliance

Your plan aligns with Berkeley EMBER requirements, CAL FIRE standards, and Fire Safe guidelines. Here's what applies to your property.

Berkeley EMBER Program Requirements
▸
Zone 0 Clearance (5-foot defensible space)
All dead leaves, needles, and debris must be removed from roof, gutters, and ground within 5 feet of all buildings. This is required under Berkeley EMBER guidelines.
Status: Phase 1 addresses this requirement
Vent Screening (1/8-inch mesh)
All attic, foundation, and dryer vents must be fitted with non-corrodible metal mesh screens (1/8-inch nominal opening). Screening prevents ember entry during wildfire.
Status: Phase 1 addresses this requirement
Roof Condition & Class A Rating
Roofs must be in good repair with no missing or damaged shingles. Class A fire-rated roofing is highly recommended and may be required for permit work. Metal, tile, or specific fire-rated asphalt products qualify.
Status: Phase 1 (inspection), Phase 2 (replacement if needed)
Tree Trimming (Zone 1)
Trees within 30 feet of structures must have branches removed that hang over roofs. Lower limbs must be pruned 6-10 feet up. Trees must be spaced 10+ feet apart (crown to crown).
Status: Phase 1 (initial), Phase 2 (comprehensive)
Dead Tree Removal
Any tree that is dead, dying, or poses a hazard must be removed. This includes trees with no green foliage, significant bark damage, or obvious disease. Not an optional measure — safety critical.
Status: Phase 2 addresses this requirement
CAL FIRE Defensible Space Standards
▸
Zone 0 (Immediate — 0 to 5 feet)
Remove all dead branches, leaves, needles, and debris from roof and ground. Trim branches 6-10 feet up on nearby trees. Remove shrubs within 5 feet of house. No dead plants or plant material.
Status: Phase 1 addresses Zone 0 completely
Zone 1 (Intermediate — 5 to 30 feet)
Prune tree limbs 6-10 feet up. Remove dead or dying trees. Remove shrubs. Thin trees to 10-12 foot spacing. No ladder fuels (vegetation that allows fire to climb into tree canopies).
Status: Phase 2 addresses Zone 1 comprehensively
Zone 2 (Extended — 30 to 100+ feet)
Remove dead trees, thin overstocked forest. Maintain 10-12 foot spacing. Remove branches 6-10 feet up. Remove accumulated fuel (chips, needles, fallen branches). This zone extends defensible space and reduces wildland fuels.
Status: Phase 3 addresses Zone 2 and beyond
Fire-Safe Building Materials (Title 24 / Building Code)
▸
Class A Roofing Materials
Only Class A fire-rated materials are permitted. Options include: metal roofing (standing seam or shingles), Class A architectural shingles, concrete tiles, clay tiles, or slate. Wood shingles do NOT meet Class A standard and are prohibited in fire zones.
Status: Phase 2 roof upgrade ensures compliance
Exterior Siding & Trim
Fiber cement board, stucco, masonry, or metal siding with fire-rated backing are required. Vinyl, untreated wood, and cedar shake are not permitted in high fire-risk areas. Review local Title 24 standards for your specific zone.
Status: Phase 2 includes siding inspection and upgrades
Vent Openings & Screens
All vents (attic, dryer, foundation) must be fitted with non-corrodible 1/8-inch metal mesh. No solid closures allowed. Screens must be removable for cleaning and inspection.
Status: Phase 1 addresses all vent screening
Windows & Glass
Dual-pane or tempered windows are highly recommended. All glass penetrations (windows, doors, skylights) should be sealed with fire-rated caulk. This minimizes ember entry through gaps.
Status: Phase 2 includes window sealing and upgrades
